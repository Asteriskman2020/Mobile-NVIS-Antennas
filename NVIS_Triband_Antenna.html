DPDT Relay Wiring — Turn Switching

The two circular loops are stacked coaxially with a permanent series jumper soldered on one side of the gap. On the opposite side (cap gap), a single DPDT relay switches between 2-turn (80m) and 1-turn (40m/20m) operation.

Physical Layout at Gap

A = Loop 1 gap-end (bottom loop, left)
B = Loop 1 gap-end (bottom loop, right)
C = Loop 2 gap-end (top loop, left)
D = Loop 2 gap-end (top loop, right)
Jumper = B↔C permanently soldered (opposite side)

DPDT Relay Pinout

Pole 1 COM = Variable cap terminal 1
Pole 1 NO = D (Loop 2 gap-end right)
Pole 1 NC = B (Loop 1 gap-end right)
Pole 2 COM = D (Loop 2 gap-end right)
Pole 2 NO = open (not connected)
Pole 2 NC = B (shorts Loop 2)

80m — Relay ON (2-Turn Series)

Pole 1 energized → connects Cap to D
Both turns in series — inductance ×4 → resonates at 3.5–4.0 MHz

40m/20m — Relay OFF (1-Turn)

Pole 1 rests → connects Cap to B
Pole 2 rests → shorts D to B (kills Loop 2)
Single turn — inductance ÷4 → resonates at 7–14 MHz

Key Insight: With 2T→1T switching, inductance drops ~4×, so resonant frequency doubles. The same variable cap setting (~190 pF) tunes 80m in 2-turn mode and 40m in 1-turn mode. Only 20m requires a different cap position (~47 pF). This eliminates the need for a fixed cap or second relay.

Why short Loop 2? In 1-turn mode, Pole 2 NC shorts D→B, making Loop 2 a closed zero-area loop. This prevents Loop 2 from acting as a parasitic resonator that could detune Loop 1.

Build & Installation Guide

1

Form 2 Circular Loops

Cut 2× 3.77 m of 40 mm Cu tube. Silver-plate (electroless). Fill with sand, bend into Ø 1.20 m circles. Leave 20 mm gap for capacitor leads.

2

Series Jumper (50 mm)

Stack loops coaxially, 50 mm apart. Silver-solder 50 mm Cu jumper on one side. Use PVC spacers on 4 posts.

3

Coupling Loop

Bend 78 cm of RG-213 into 240 mm Faraday loop. Mount at bottom of main loop. Shield both ends, center conductor one end. SO-239 connector.

4

Variable Vacuum Cap

In IP65 box: Vacuum variable 20–250 pF, 7.5 kV, Q ≈ 30,000 (stepper-driven). Covers all 3 bands — no fixed cap needed.

5

DPDT Turn-Switch Relay

Single DPDT relay switches turns only (no cap relay needed). ON = 80m (2-Turn). OFF = 40m/20m (1-Turn).

6

Mag-Mount Install

4× NdFeB 40 kg mounts on clean sedan roof. 80 mm HDPE posts + 50 mm extension clips for Turn 2. Total profile ~13 cm.

7

Tri-Band Operation

ESP32 selects band: 80m → relay ON + stepper tune. 40m/20m → relay OFF + stepper to ~190 pF or ~47 pF. Bandwidth: 2.5/9/12 kHz.
